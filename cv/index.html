For 14 years my work as an academic librarian has focused on making it easier to find, use, and re-purpose digital scholarship, especially ebooks and innovative digital publications. I specialize in front end web development, user research, product and interface design, web and ebook accessibility, and accessible digital publishing workflows for academic publishers.

Professional Employment

Digital Product Design Engineer & Accessibility Specialist, Michigan Publishing, University of Michigan Library, Ann Arbor, 2021-present
Front End Developer, 2013-21

  • Lead UX and user research, UI design, accessibility, and front end development for Fulcrum, a Andrew W. Mellon-funded (Phase I, Phase II, Phase III) open-source publishing platform and set of services that enable the publication and preservation of digitally enriched monographs. The platform enables publishing books in EPUB3 and PDF along with presenting and preserving their associated resources in Samvera-powered Fedora repositories.
    • Since launching in 2016, Fulcrum has onboarded 25 publishing partners, grown to a collection of over 15,000 scholarly ebooks, 37,000 media files, has around 700,000 active users per year from 227 countries, and licenses content to nearly 900 libraries across the world with an annual revenue of around $2.3 million.
    • To help center product development around users and stakeholders, I built a user research program from the ground-up that has conducted user testing, focus groups, targeted surveys and gathered feedback from over 13,000 participants.
  • Lead organizational digital accessibility initiatives at Michigan Publishing and University of Michigan Library, including chairing the Accessible Publishing Working Group and leading University of Michigan Press's design and implementation of an accessible publishing workflow.
    • My work developing a born-accessible workflow for the U-M Press enabled it to become the first university press to receive Benetech's Global Certified Accessible certification in 2021 and has been recertified annually since.
    • I've led workshops and been invited to present at numerous library and publishing conferences on our accessibility work, advocating for inclusive design practices and publishing workflows and influencing the scholarly publishing community's adoption of born-accessible practices.
    • I was recognized for significant leadership and efforts in digital accessibility by the University of Michigan in 2025, receiving honorable mention for the James T. Neubacher award.
  • Lead front end development, UI design, user research, and accessibility for over 30 web assets including publisher websites and digital monographs hosted hosted on a variety of platforms with a variety of internal and external stakeholders.
  • Develop scripts, write documentation, and improve workflows for converting and publishing content in specific XML schemas, HTML5, and EPUB3.
  • Created Digital Product Design Assistant graduate student position in 2020; hired, supervised, and mentored seven U-M School of Information graduate students seeking careers in UX and user research.

  • Project manager for the development, creation, and management of the NEH, CDC, and Robert Wood Johnson Foundation funded The American Influenza Epidemic of 1918-1919: A Digital Encyclopedia. The project consists of a collection of over 16,000 freely available historical documents and photographs complemented with essays exploring how various U.S. cities reacted to the 1918 influenza epidemic. This project was highly utilized by journalists, the general public and researchers throughout the recent global pandemic.
  • Managed the web conversion and publication processes for a stable of open access journals published and hosted by Michigan Publishing.
  • Developed scripts and improved workflows for converting and publishing content in specific XML schemas.

  • Project manager for Wayne State University Institutional Repository and activities; provided the strategic planning, day-to-day management, staff/department education, marketing and promotion for WSU’s institutional repository.
  • Collaborated with WSU Press to plan, implement, and manage online subscription based journals program; managed and developed back-end work flow to migrate 6 peer-reviewed journals to Digital Commons; created workshops and training for editorial staff; developed digital publishing internship.
  • Created and led the WSU Scholarly Communications Team, managing projects, outreach, marketing, and other scholarly communications initiatives; coordinated and led first WSU Open Access Week.
  • Researched, planned, implemented, and manage open access theses and dissertations program; copyright and permissions management; collaboration with Graduate School, ProQuest, and library departments; established data transformation workflows, involving XSLT transformations of XML.
  • Researched, planned, implemented, and manage faculty self-archiving program; developed workflows and services to handle permissions and rights clearance, document reformatting, and publication deposits to Digital Commons for faculty and staff; hired, trained, and supervised two undergraduate and two graduate interns; developed workshops on self-archiving and author’s rights for librarians, faculty, and graduate students.

  • Managed digital collection of over 300,000 digital images, developing web-services to improve access and usability.
  • Collaborated with the Smithsonian’s Freer Sackler Gallery, developing and implementing a digital project that brought the Peacock Room and its collection to a digital environment.
  • Project manager for NEH funded grant to build presentation tools for digital collection software.
  • Assisted in the development and maintenance of Wayne State University Library’s website, including a large-scale project to assess, re-design, build, and implement a new library website.
  • Project manager for Wayne State University Institutional Repository and activities; provided the strategic planning, day-to-day management, staff/department education, marketing and promotion for WSU’s institutional repository.

Providing small-scale web design, development, and consulting services for publishers, academic digital humanities projects, and other digital publishing ventures.
